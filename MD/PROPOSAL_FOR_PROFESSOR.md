# Cross-Modal Prompt Injection in Omni-Modal LLMs: Characterization and a Fusion-Only Attack

**COSMOS Cluster 6 capstone proposal.** Defensive / educational; all experiments use benign proxies, no harmful content.

## Abstract
Omni-modal LLMs (image + audio + text in one model) are safety-tuned to refuse malicious instructions. We ask whether that safety is *uniform across modalities* and find, on a locally-run Qwen2.5-Omni, that it is **not**: an instruction the model **refuses as text** is **obeyed** when the identical instruction is delivered through the **vision encoder** (the audio channel shows a weaker, prompt-sensitive effect; under the de-confounded prompt the full spoken instruction is refused). We trace this to the input pipeline — image/audio become **continuous encoder embeddings** scattered into the token sequence, bypassing the text-token space alignment was trained on — and rule out position and content via controlled A/B. We then (i) map the attack surface (which carriers succeed and *why*, including a **degradation-robust, human-imperceptible** faint-text image), (ii) catch and control a **scoring confound** that inflates naive measurements, and (iii) propose a new attack — **fusion-only injection**: jointly-optimized imperceptible image + audio perturbations whose malicious behavior emerges **only from their fusion**, each channel individually inert *and* transcribing benign. We hypothesize such an attack *would be* invisible to content-recovery per-channel defenses by construction, and — *if* it also evades per-channel adversarial detectors (CIDER/E²AT) — *would* motivate joint cross-modal detection (unstudied for image+audio). This is gated on the Rung 2.0 feasibility result.

## Feasibility — already confirmed (single 16 GB GPU, no cloud)
- **Rig:** Qwen2.5-Omni-3B/7B via `transformers` on an RTX 5080; audio input verified end-to-end.
- **Safety is text-only (the central finding).** On the 7B the identical malicious instruction is **refused as text** but **leaks via the image encoder (4/4 trials)**. A controlled A/B (same sentence, same sequence position, only the channel differs) **rules out position and content** — an **encoder-path effect**. The audio leg is weaker: under the de-confounded prompt the full spoken instruction is refused (the earlier "audio 2/4" came from a confounded run, withdrawn pending clean re-measurement). Token construction dumped (image → 429 embedding tokens, audio → 32).
- **Attack surface mapped.** A faint-text image at **1/255 contrast** (invisible to a human) reliably injects and is **robust to JPEG, downscaling, and simulated re-photography (25/25)**. Ultrasonic, LSB, EXIF, and DCT-domain steganography are **provably dead** against the pixel/16 kHz front-ends. A text-channel **special-token role-forgery** also succeeds.
- **A methodological confound caught & controlled.** A contentless image plus an instruction-referencing prompt weakens the guard on its own; a benign-disambiguation control separates genuine carriers from this artifact — tightening every downstream claim.
- **A scope boundary established.** Content-recovery defenses catch **symbolic** injections but are blind to **sub-symbolic** adversarial-perturbation attacks (AudioHijack); a negative result — the model cannot perform fine **temporal** audio↔video binding — rules out a temporal-synchronization attack class on this model.

## Contribution & next steps
**Primary — fusion-only injection.** Jointly optimize a small image perturbation and a small audio perturbation on Qwen2.5-Omni such that the malicious behavior arises **only from the fused input**, with each channel *alone* (a) inert and (b) captioning/transcribing to benign content. Because the payload would exist only in the **joint embedding geometry**, a content-recovery per-channel defense cannot recover it by construction; whether it *also* evades per-channel **adversarial** detectors (CIDER/E²AT) is the open empirical question this attack aims to answer — if so, detection would have to be joint. Unlike the closest prior work (which uses a *visible text* channel), it would leave no per-channel content signal. (Qwen2.5-Omni's audio path is continuous/differentiable, so standard PGD suffices.)
1. Build the joint-optimization harness on the 3B; success = high joint attack-success rate with near-zero single-channel rate. *verify: fusion-only.*
2. Add the transcribe-benign constraint; run per-channel detectors (content-recovery *and* adversarial) and measure evasion. *verify: recall ≈ 0 on fusion-only vs high on the symbolic split — noting the content-recovery miss is definitional; the adversarial-detector evasion is the load-bearing empirical result.*
3. (Stretch) prototype the **only** defense that can catch it — a joint/fusion-level consistency check.
4. Package the full characterization as the study backbone; assemble the poster.

**Honest scope of novelty.** The *phenomenon* (safety fails on non-text channels) is now documented in the literature; our novel deltas are (i) the **fusion-only image+audio** attack — aiming to defeat content-recovery by construction and per-channel adversarial detectors empirically (motivating joint detection), and (ii) the **systematic attack-surface characterization** with the confound control and the symbolic/sub-symbolic boundary.

## Prior literature & positioning
- **Cross-modal / multimodal attacks:** *Jailbreak in Pieces* (Shayegani et al., ICLR 2024, arXiv:2307.14539) — split-payload image+text; *Abusing Images and Sounds* (Bagdasaryan et al., arXiv:2307.10490) — image *or* audio as single vectors; *CrossInject* (ACM MM 2025, arXiv:2504.14348); *JAMA* (INTERSPEECH 2026, arXiv:2603.19127) — joint audio **+ text** adversarial (closest to ours; its text channel is moderator-visible); *AudioHijack* (IEEE S&P 2026, arXiv:2604.14604) — imperceptible single-channel adversarial audio (the sub-symbolic boundary our detector cannot cross).
- **Modality safety-generalization gap:** *The Alignment Curse* (arXiv:2602.02557) — documents the text-vs-audio gap on Qwen2.5-Omni-3B/7B (our core phenomenon is known); *CMRM* (ICLR 2025, arXiv:2410.09047); *Words or Vision: Do VLMs Have Blind Faith in Text?* (CVPR 2025, arXiv:2503.02199) — text dominance.
- **Defenses (consistency / detection):** *Wu et al.* (ICLR 2025, arXiv:2406.12814) — self-captioning consistency (image+text); *CIDER* (EMNLP 2024, arXiv:2407.21659); *Omni-SafetyBench / CMSC* (arXiv:2508.07173) — cross-modal-consistency as a *metric*; *MELON* (ICML 2025, arXiv:2502.05174); *Attention Tracker* (arXiv:2411.00348) — attention-level (image+text); *DefenSee* (arXiv:2512.01185) — cross-modal-consistency + image-variants transcription (black-box, multi-modal); *E²AT* (arXiv:2503.04833) — joint-optimization multimodal defense. These establish lift-to-text and fused-vs-marginal detection for image+text — which is *why* our contribution is the attack that defeats all of them at once.
- **Model & mechanism:** Qwen2.5-Omni report (arXiv:2503.20215; TMRoPE, Thinker–Talker); text-only refusal-direction / "safety heads" work motivates but doesn't cover the multimodal case.

*Novelty caveat: "not found in careful search" is not proof of absence; load-bearing citations are verified against primary sources before use.*
